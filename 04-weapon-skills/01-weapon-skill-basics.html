01 - Weapon Skill Basics

With each level up, a player can choose to learn a new ability that they qualify for. Weapon Skills are among the abilities that can be learned.

Weapon Skills grant players who like to rely more on physical combat a choice in how they want to specialize their character. Different weapons have different strengths and weaknesses! Some Weapon Skills are Active Abilities that can be used in a manner similar to Magic, while others are passive perks that make your favored weapon more effective.

Proposed weapon types:

  • Light weapons (Daggers, fists)
  • Medium Weapons (Swords, hand axes)
  • Heavy weapons (Greataxes, Giant Hammers)
  • Reach Weapons (Spears, Whips)
  • Ranged weapons (Bows, guns)

At level 3, and every 3 levels after (3, 6, 9, etc), you can choose a weapon type to gain additional proficiency in. Each rank of proficiency adds an extra bonus when you make a standard attack with that weapon type.

You automatically gain the first proficiency skill with your class (which adds +1 die to your attacks with that weapon). You can choose to 'favor' a weapon that isn't favored by default by your class. So for example, rogues start with proficiency 1 with daggers since they're their class's favored weapon. When they reach level 3, they can choose to favor a new weapon by ranking that weapon's proficiency to 1, instead of leveling dagger proficiency to 2.

Daggers

Daggers are fast-striking weapons that specialize in multi-attacks and causing status effects. They're most commonly wielded by rogues, but monks and fighters can use them well, too.

Daggers deal +2 damage dice.

Proficiency Bonuses

Proficiency 1: Favored Weapon

Favored Weapons gain +1 die to attack rolls.

Proficiency 2: Multi-Attack

If you roll a 6 on your standard attack, make a second attack attempt (whether the initial attack hits or not). This can only occur once per turn.

Proficiency 3: Exploit Weakness

By exploiting small gaps in the enemy's defenses, you impose a -1 die penalty on their STA defense rolls.

Proficiency 4: Chained Multi-Attack

Multi-Attack can now trigger twice in one turn.

Active Skills

Bleed (2AP)

Deal a quick strike that causes bleeding. If the attack hits, place 3 bleed tokens on the target.

Counter Attack (1AP)

Only usable immediately after successfully dodging an attack. Attempt a standard attack against the attacker, provided they're within range to do so. Can only occur once per round.

Flicker (2AP)

Roll your AGI against a target in melee range. If you succeed, you perform a feint that distracts them, reducing their defense dice by 1 until your next turn.
